Empowering Disabled People

Disability is part of being human. An estimated 1.3 billion people – about 16% of the global population – currently experience significant disability.

How to Support Disabled People

  • Use Respectful Language: Be mindful of your language when discussing disability. Use person-first language (e.g., "people with disabilities") or identity-first language (e.g., "disabled person") based on individual preferences.
  • Volunteer and Assist: Offer your time to help disabled individuals. Volunteer to assist with transportation, run errands, or provide companionship.
  • Educate Others: Educate friends, family, and colleagues about disability awareness and inclusion. Share information online or in person to raise awareness and promote understanding.

Remember, small acts of kindness and understanding can make a big difference in empowering disabled people!
